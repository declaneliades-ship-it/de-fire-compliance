What Happens If Your Fire Doors Aren’t Compliant?

Non-compliant fire doors can lead to serious consequences for the responsible person. We help you identify issues early and stay compliant with confidence.

  • Failed Audits Non-compliant fire doors are the most common finding in fire risk assessments.
  • Enforcement Notices Fire authorities can issue enforcement and prohibition notices, requiring immediate action.
  • Personal Legal Liability The responsible person can face fines or criminal prosecution for fire safety failures.
  • Insurance Complications Insurers may reduce or refuse claims if fire doors are found to be non-compliant.

A compartmentation survey assesses the fire-resisting barriers within a building — walls, floors, ceilings, service penetrations, and cavity barriers. The purpose is to verify that fire and smoke cannot spread between compartments. We identify breaches, document them with photographic evidence, and provide remedial recommendations with severity ratings.
